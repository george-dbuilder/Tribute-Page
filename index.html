A Covid-19 Tribute Page

To doctors, nurses and every other health worker who died from Covid-19 while on duty

A tribute to remember those who lost their lives working in hospitals and care homes during the coronavirus pandemic.

More than 25,000 healthcare workers have contracted the coronavirus.

Many doctors, nurses, EMTs, and support staff had been forced to reuse their personal protective equipment during their shifts, making them especially susceptible to the virus.

As front-line healthcare workers care for patients with COVID-19, they commit themselves to difficult, draining work and also put themselves at risk of infection. Thousands throughout the world have died.

Doctors, nurses, carers, porters, cleaners and paramedics are among those who have died after being infected with COVID-19.

Behind each number is the death of a beloved family member and colleague.

Their sacrifice would forever not be forgotten.

With that in mind, we pay tribute to these people who have died and tell their stories.

Would you like to share a story about a friend or relative you have lost? Whether it's to pay tribute or share a funny story about their lives, we want to hear from you. We will be featuring stories of lost loved ones sent to us by readers to remember the many people who have sadly lost their lives to the virus.
